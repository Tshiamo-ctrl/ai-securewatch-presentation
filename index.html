Problem space

The gap between ERP records and financial reality

Most organisations have two pictures of their payments: what their systems believe happened, and what actually happened in the bank account.

ERP, AP automation, vendor master files and TPRM platforms are all built around the approved vendor list and in‑system transactions, so they struggle to surface entities that receive funds without ever being set up as vendors.

This blind spot enables ghost vendor fraud and “shadow” entities — legitimate‑looking payment recipients that sit outside formal procurement and risk workflows.

Key pain themes

  • Boards and audit committees increasingly ask for independent proof of who actually received payment, not just ERP reports.
  • Ghost vendor schemes and social‑engineering frauds continue to hit public bodies and enterprises, with multi‑million‑dollar losses.
  • Traditional AP and audit approaches still rely heavily on sampling rather than full‑population testing of all payees.
  • Third‑party risk tools focus on registered vendors and questionnaire workflows, not the long tail of entities appearing only in payment data.
Market context

Where AI SecureWatch sits in the risk & assurance stack

AI SecureWatch is an enterprise intelligence layer focused on “ground truth” for payments — ingesting AP files and the vendor master to produce a reconciled view of every entity that actually received money, and flagging those that are not in the master file.

That puts the product at the intersection of accounts payable audit, forensic investigation, and third‑party risk management rather than competing directly with ERP or AP automation platforms.

Primary buyers
Internal Audit · TPRM · CFO

The deck explicitly calls out Heads of Internal Audit, third‑party risk leaders and CFOs/finance executives as the three core buyer personas.

Service model
Pilot → License

A paid pilot on 12–36 months of AP data with a single findings report, converting into an annual license with recurring runs for continuous monitoring.

Delivery
2–3 weeks

From receiving AP exports and the vendor master, AI‑driven reconciliation plus human analyst QA produces the out‑of‑master entity list in roughly 2–3 weeks.

FSG Forensics, a South African forensic investigation practice founded in 1985, appears as a delivery partner, giving AI SecureWatch access to established forensic capabilities and credibility with enterprise and public‑sector clients.

Brand & GTM narrative

How I would tell the story to enterprise buyers

Brand idea · “Ground truth for payments”

The most distinctive and defensible idea in the deck is the focus on “financial reality” — a complete, system‑independent picture of every entity that actually received payment in the last 12 months.

That creates a clean, board‑friendly promise: you are not buying another dashboard; you are buying a reconciled, investigation‑ready list of entities that sit outside the vendor master but still receive funds.

One question: “Who actually got paid?” Single findings report as core deliverable Independent of ERP and TPRM stack

GTM angle · “Stop sampling, see 100%”

Most AP audits and TPRM assessments still rely on sampling vendors and invoices; the AI SecureWatch offer is to reconcile the full population and hand over a prioritised out‑of‑master list tied to actual spend.

For internal audit and risk leaders, this reframes the product from a tool to a targeted assurance engagement that immediately feeds their investigation pipeline.

Pilot anchored in a real AP export Quantified exposure (entities × spend) Hand‑off to forensic partners like FSG

Conversation structure for buyers

  • Start from recent, public fraud examples (e.g. councils losing ~USD 1.9m to AI‑driven vendor fraud) to establish that current controls can be bypassed without system breaches.
  • Ask whether the organisation can produce, on demand, a complete list of all entities that received payments last year, independent of ERP and vendor master constraints.
  • Position AI SecureWatch as a low‑friction, export‑only pilot that answers that question once, with clear numbers and a finite out‑of‑master list.
  • Show how that list slots into existing internal audit, TPRM and forensic investigation workflows, rather than asking teams to adopt a new platform.

Signals to keep in the brand

  • System‑independent, file‑based delivery (no credentials, no ERP integration).
  • AI reconciliation plus human analyst QA — no raw model output given directly to clients.
  • Partnership with an established forensic firm for follow‑through, not just detection.
  • Calm, insight‑led language that avoids fear‑mongering and emphasises visibility and governance.
