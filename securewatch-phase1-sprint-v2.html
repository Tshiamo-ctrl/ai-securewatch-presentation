Product roadmap

Where Phase 1 sits in the build-out.

Stage 1 pilots → Stage 2 licences → Stage 3 partner resell
This mirrors your business plan: harden the pilot motion, then industrialise the platform and expand through partners. YC and enterprise buyers both want to see this line of sight.
Stage 1
Paid pilots (now)
Goal: prove value & close first 3–5 clients
  • Run time-boxed pilots (12–36 months AP) with analyst-reviewed reports.
  • Quantify ghost vendors, duplicates and anomaly exposure per client.
  • Secure written references and anonymised case studies where justified.
Stage 2
Annual licences
Goal: recurring monitoring & ARR
  • Industrialise the engine into a hardened, multi-entity platform.
  • Introduce quarterly or monthly reconciliation cycles per entity.
  • Expose dashboards, alerts, exports and findings APIs into audit & GRC.
Stage 3
Partner resell & white-label
Goal: scale via forensic & audit partners
  • Formalise co-sell and revenue-share with firms like FSG and audit practices.
  • Offer white-label vendor-assurance services on top of the platform.
  • Add advanced modules (entity resolution, live ERP feeds, bank reconciliation) where demand and ROI justify.
API & enterprise

How this becomes an enterprise-grade layer.

Designed for banks, SOEs & complex AP environments
Phase 1 deliberately stays file-based to minimise integration friction. For larger, multi-ERP enterprises, your roadmap already calls for standard ingestion pipelines, direct ERP connectivity, and interfaces into audit and GRC systems; this section makes that evolution explicit without overselling today's stack.
Package

Ingestion API

Read-only endpoints or managed connectors that accept AP and vendor-master data from SAP, Oracle, Sage and other ERPs on a schedule.

Stage: Stage 2 roadmap (post-pilot) For: Clients with multiple ERPs or shared-services centres who want automation instead of manual exports.
Package

Findings & evidence API

Secure feeds exposing confirmed findings, vendor flags and evidence chains for ingestion into audit management, case management or GRC platforms.

Stage: Stage 2–3 roadmap For: Internal Audit, Forensics and Risk teams who want findings inside existing tooling.
Package

Bank & registry integrations

Optional modules for bank-statement reconciliation and CIPC or other registry lookups to enrich vendor risk and confirm actual cash outflows.

Stage: Stage 3 roadmap For: High-risk sectors or post-incident reviews where bank-level tracing and director checks are mandatory.
YC & traction

What YC and serious clients see at a glance.

Evidence-first story, not feature claims
The same artefacts that de-risk a pilot for a CFO or CAE also answer YC's core questions: what problem you're solving, why now, what you've already proven, and how you scale.
  • Problem & wedge
    Most organisations cannot answer “who exactly received money in the last 12–36 months?” because ERP controls, audit sampling and TPRM all assume the vendor master is complete.
  • Solution
    A system-independent reconciliation layer that compares AP payment files with vendor masters to surface every entity that received money but is not in the master, plus high-risk patterns inside the master itself.
  • Why now
    Boards are demanding financial ground truth post-scandals; AI makes full-population reconciliation practical; growing ERP complexity guarantees permanent blind spots.
  • Traction & pipeline
    1 live Sage 200 pilot (Technomobile), 1 anonymised construction pilot in deck, 40+ discovery calls, 10+ hot leads, and an FSG co-sell partnership targeting government, SOEs and financial services.
  • Defensibility
    System independence, full-population reconciliation (no sampling), human analyst on every flag, and a forensic partner that can act on findings.
  • This sprint's YC output
    YC memo and metrics snapshot, a short founder video script, and this sprint site as a live artefact showing you can move from prototype to repeatable pilot delivery.
